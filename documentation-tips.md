# How to Document Your Project

Good documentation is part of the design work, not an afterthought. Different outcomes need different methods — here are a few options.
Most projects benefit from a combination of documentation methods rather than just one. Make sure to take good photos during exhibition moments. 


## Image / Video Export

Best for:
* Posters
* Identity systems
* Graphics / illustrations
* Animations

Tips:
* Export at the correct dimensions and resolution.
* Think about what you want to show besides the final result, e.g. the styleguide or concept for a CI or a detail of a poster. 


## Screenshot

Best for:
* Websites
* Interfaces
* Digital publications

Tips:
* Use Safari or another browser with a clean interface.
* Hide bookmarks, tabs, and extensions.
* Set your browser window to the intended screen size before capturing.
* Consider using a simple background color that complements your project.
* Use multiple screenshots to display an interaction or animation.
* For presentations or portfolios, you can also place screenshots inside browser mockups.

### macOS

* `⌘ + Shift + 4` — capture a selected area
* `⌘ + Shift + 4`, then `Space` — capture a single window with a clean shadow
* `⌘ + Shift + 5` — opens the screenshot and screen recording toolbar

### Windows

* `Win + Shift + S` — Snipping Tool for area, window, or fullscreen captures

## Mockup

Best for:
* Digital interfaces
* Branding
* Editorial design

<p align="center"> <img width="500" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/00f8840c-4038-49cb-9f6f-38cde4788758" /> </p>

Tips: 
* Either choose a very simple mockup or an interesting one.
* Don’t overuse mockups. A photo or scan often looks better than a mockup and makes your project more real.
* Perhaps combine a mockup with photos to show your project also exists in real life.

## Photography

Best for:
* Printed matter
* Installations
* Exhibitions
* Posters

Tips:
* Use a proper camera and tripod whenever possible.
* Work with natural, even lighting or use a photo studio.
* Photograph details as well as overall views.
* Ask a photographer friend for help.

## Scan

Best for:
* Sketches
* Printed matter
* Drawings
* Books and magazines
* Material samples

Tips:
* Use a flatbed scanner whenever possible.
* Scan at **300 dpi** for general documentation.
* Use **600 dpi or higher** if you expect viewers to zoom into small typography, textures, or illustrations.

## Screencast

Best for:
* Websites
* Interactive prototypes
* Motion graphics
* Generative design
* Digital tools

Tips:
* Use Safari or another browser with a clean interface.
* Hide bookmarks, tabs, and extensions.
* Set the recording window to the intended size & resolution.
* Consider using a simple background color that complements your project.
* Plan the interaction before recording.
* Record slowly and deliberately—avoid rushed mouse movements.
* Keep clips short (10–30 seconds is often enough).

### macOS

* `⌘ + Shift + 5` → **Record Entire Screen** or **Record Selected Portion**
* Or open **QuickTime Player** → **File → New Screen Recording**
